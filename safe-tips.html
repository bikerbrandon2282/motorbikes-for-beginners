Safety Tips

First things first, You need the right gear and awareness to stay safe on the road. Below are the basic types of gear you should wear in order to protect yourself on the road.

Essential gear

  • Helmet: Always wear a DOT or ECE certified helmet to protect your head in case of an accident.
  • Jacket: A motorcycle jacket with armor can protect your upper body from injuries.
  • Gloves: Motorcycle gloves provide protection for your hands and improve grip.
  • Pants: Motorcycle pants with armor can protect your legs from injuries.
  • Boots: Sturdy motorcycle boots can protect your feet and ankles.

Ride to the conditions of the road

Unfortunately, when you are riding a motorcycle on the road, you are essentially invisible to other road users, So there are some tips you should be aware of in order to protect yourself for potential accidents.

Always assume that other drivers cannot see you and ride defensively. Be especially cautious at intersections, where many accidents occur. Use your mirrors frequently and be aware of blind spots. Avoid riding in bad weather conditions if possible, as it can reduce visibility and increase the risk of accidents.

Road Awareness

Here are some tips you should be aware of in order to protect yourself for potential accidents.

  • Be cautious around intersections as this is where many accidents occur.
  • Use your mirrors frequently and be aware of blind spots.
  • If you like to ride all year round you will ride in different weather conditions with various new hazards that are not present in ideal conditions.
    So be aware of your surroundings, plan ahead and ride defensively.

Maintenance

Proper maintenance is crucial for the safety and performance of your motorcycle. Here are a few tips to get you started.

Some essential maintenance tasks include:

  • Regularly check and change your oil to keep your engine running smoothly. (Oil change every 6-12 months depending how often you ride)
  • Inspect your tires for wear and maintain proper tire pressure for better handling and safety.
  • Check your brakes regularly and replace brake pads when necessary to ensure optimal stopping power.
  • Keep your chain clean and lubricated to prevent it from wearing out prematurely. (every 400-600 miles)
  • Regularly inspect your lights and signals to ensure they are functioning properly for visibility and safety.
